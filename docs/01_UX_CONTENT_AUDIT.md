# 01 — UX and Content Audit

## Current-site diagnosis

The public site provides a usable starting point, but the redesign must correct the following structural risks:

### Visual hierarchy

- The palette relies too heavily on pink tones.
- Contrast is insufficient in areas where pale backgrounds and light text meet.
- Decorative color competes with information.
- The logo and brand identity need a more intentional presence.
- Section rhythm should be simplified so the page feels editorial rather than assembled from unrelated blocks.

### Content hierarchy

- The value proposition must be clearer above the fold.
- Treatments should be grouped by user need or category instead of appearing as an undifferentiated list.
- Trust-building information should appear before the visitor is asked to commit.
- Calls to action should use consistent labels and appear at deliberate decision points.
- Long text should be rewritten into short, scannable blocks.

### Conversion

- WhatsApp and appointment actions must be persistent but not intrusive.
- The page should make the next step obvious after each major section.
- Location, business hours and contact details should be easy to find.
- Social proof must be genuine and attributable.

### Mobile

- The redesign must prioritize one-column reading.
- No text should sit over visually busy images without a strong overlay.
- Tap targets must be at least 44 px.
- Sticky elements must not cover content or interfere with browser controls.

## Required source inventory

Before final copy or UI implementation, capture from the current site:

- exact business name and logo variants;
- treatment names and descriptions;
- WhatsApp number and generated link;
- Instagram and other social URLs;
- physical address and map link;
- opening hours;
- email address;
- all public images and their likely ownership/source;
- current legal notices;
- any verified professional names, roles and credentials;
- all existing form fields and destinations.

Store findings in `docs/SOURCE_INVENTORY.md`.

## Content gaps requiring owner confirmation

The following must never be invented:

- number of patients or years of experience;
- satisfaction percentages;
- medical registrations;
- professional titles;
- equipment brands;
- financing and accepted payment methods;
- treatment prices;
- testimonials;
- before/after permissions;
- guarantees or expected duration of results.

Record missing information in `docs/CONTENT_GAPS.md`.

## Recommended copy direction

### Hero concept

**Headline:** Belleza cuidada, resultados naturales.

**Supporting line:** Tratamientos estéticos personalizados en Palermo Soho, pensados para acompañar tus objetivos con una atención cercana y profesional.

This is a working direction, not final approved copy. It must be validated against the actual service model and professional credentials.

### Tone

- calm;
- confident;
- specific;
- human;
- non-technical unless necessary;
- free of exaggerated claims.

## Priority improvements

1. Replace low-contrast pink with a neutral premium system.
2. Rewrite hero and establish one clear primary CTA.
3. Reorganize treatments into logical categories.
4. Add verified trust signals.
5. Improve logo scale, whitespace and header behavior.
6. Build a strong location/contact close.
7. Validate all content and legal claims before launch.
